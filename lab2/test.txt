Information entropy is a measure of uncertainty or randomness in a data set. When all symbols are equally 
likely to occur, the entropy is at its maximum. When one symbol appears much more frequently than others, 
the entropy is low. Shannon entropy is calculated as the negative sum of probabilities times log base two 
of probabilities. This concept is fundamental to information theory and data compression. The more random 
the data, the harder it is to compress because each symbol carries more information.
